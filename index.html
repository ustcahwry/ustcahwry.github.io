Transciptome Profile Reveals a new Cyclin-CYC 2 function in DNA repair during meiosis in Tetrahymena themorphila

Knocking out Tetrahymena thermophila strain of CYC2 gene paused at about 3hr during the conjugation stage.Such dramatic phenotype makes this strain a quite proper model for studying meiosis.We perform the RNA-seq on CYC2-KO. strain and WT strain on four different time,2hr,2.5hr,3hr,3.5hr. The transcriptome profile we've performed reveals that Cyclin 2 may play an essential role in DNA mismatch repair during Tetrahymena Thermophila meiosis

Nascent RNAs Mediate Chromosomal Interactions

To address the question that whether nascent RNAs correlate with chromosomal interactions at genome scale, we analyzed whole genome chromosomal interaction data (Hi-C data) and GRO-Seq (Global Run-on Sequence) data from the same cell line (IMR90) generated by Ren Lab at UCSD. Our results showed that nascent RNAs in nuclear positively correlate with Hi-C fragments both in chromosomal interaction number and interaction frequency. This phenomenon suggests that nascent RNAs might mediate chromosomal interactions genome-wide.

Ruoyu Wang, Qi Peng, Xiaoyuan Song

The 3D chromosome conformation and transcriptional regulation of Tetrahymena thermophila

Recently, many researches revealed that chromosome interactions can regulate genes expression. Genes expression are regulated by locus control regions (LCRs) which may located far away from their target genes, but during different development or differentiation they may form long-range intra- or inter-chromosomal loops and localize genes within nuclear regions that favor transcription. We are interested in the three dimensional regulation of transcription. So we introduced Hi-C which can identify genome wide long range interactions in micronuclei of Tetrahymena thermophila.

Zhengyu Luo, Ruoyu Wang, Xiaoyuan Song
