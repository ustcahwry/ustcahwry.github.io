Research Projects and Interests

Transciptome Profile Reveals a new Cyclin-CYC 2 function in DNA repair during meiosis in Tetrahymena themorphila

Knocking out Tetrahymena thermophila strain of CYC2 gene paused at about 3hr during the conjugation stage.Such dramatic phenotype makes this strain a quite proper model for studying meiosis.We perform the RNA-seq on CYC2-KO. strain and WT strain on four different time,2hr,2.5hr,3hr,3.5hr. The transcriptome profile we've performed reveals that Cyclin 2 may play an essential role in DNA mismatch repair during Tetrahymena Thermophila meiosis

Qianlan Xu , Ruoyu Wang ,Xiaoyuan Song(Co First Author, April 2016 Protein & Cell)

Comprehensive Pan-cancer Analysis Reveals Inuslators'Role in Cancer

Insulator has been discovered for decades, but its role in human diseases like cancers remains largely unknown. Hre we perforemed a pan-cancer genome-wide analysis on TCGA cohorts, aiming to decipher the role of insulators in cancer. Utilizing computational analysis on TCGA methylation and RNA-Seq data, we have identified a wide range of both hyperemthylated and hypomethylated insulators in 18 cancer types. We successfully identified genes linked with these insulators. Survival analysis on these insulators' methylation suggested insulators' power in predicting cinical outcomes. According to our analysis results, we proposed a model for understanding insulators' role in cancer. In short, our comrehensive analysis provides new insight and resources for further mechansim study of insulators' role in cancer.

Ruoyu Wang,Xiaoyuan Song

The 3D genome organization and transcriptional regulation of Tetrahymena thermophila

Recently, many researches revealed that chromosome interactions can regulate genes expression. Genes expression are regulated by locus control regions (LCRs) which may located far away from their target genes, but during different development or differentiation they may form long-range intra- or inter-chromosomal loops and localize genes within nuclear regions that favor transcription. We are interested in the three dimensional regulation of transcription. So we introduced Hi-C which can identify genome wide long range interactions in micronuclei of Tetrahymena thermophila.

Zhengyu Luo, Ruoyu Wang, Xiaoyuan Song

High Order Chromatin Organization with Nascent RNA mediating

To address the question that whether nascent RNAs correlate with chromosomal interactions at genome scale, we analyzed whole genome chromosomal interaction data (Hi-C data) and GRO-Seq (Global Run-on Sequence) data from the same cell line (IMR90) generated by Ren Lab at UCSD. Our results showed that nascent RNAs in nuclear positively correlate with Hi-C fragments both in chromosomal interaction number and interaction frequency. This phenomenon suggests that nascent RNAs might mediate chromosomal interactions genome-wide.

Ruoyu Wang, Qi Peng, Xiaoyuan Song

Disease Associated Genetic Variations and 3D genome

Most of the diseased genetic variations happened in non-coding locus. Besides enhancer dysfunction caused by genetic variations, non-coding mutations may also contribute to high order chromatin structure reorganization. By combining Genome-wide Associated Study data and Hi-C data analysis, We've found a genetic variation loci which is highly associated with Schizophrenia located in Topological Associating Domains(TADs) boundary region. We hypothesized that this boundary region mutation could cause high order chromatin structure changed. And we are using CRISPR-Cas9 to simulate this mutation in normal neuronal cells and using 5C to test our hypothesis.

Ruoyu Wang, Ran Zhang, Xiaoyuan Song
